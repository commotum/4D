The unit of measurement is different than the scale of the displacement.
We are not required to naively limit displacements to only unit steps.

The important distinction is:
- in ( $c t, x, y, z$ ) coordinates, the event is ( $1,1,1,1$ ) in meters,
- in $(t, x, y, z)$ coordinates, the event is $(1 / c, 1,1,1)$, where the first entry is in seconds and the others are in meters.

If you do set $c=1$, then seconds and meters are identified through light-seconds, so the same event becomes simply

$$
(t, x, y, z)=(1,1,1,1)
$$

but now the time entry means 1 meter of time, i.e. $1 / c$ seconds.
So the answer depends on which coordinate convention you are using:

$$
\begin{gathered}
x^\mu=(c t, x, y, z) \quad \Rightarrow \quad(1,1,1,1) \\
x^\mu=(t, x, y, z), c \neq 1 \quad \Rightarrow \quad\left(\frac{1}{c}, 1,1,1\right) \\
x^\mu=(t, x, y, z), c=1 \quad \Rightarrow \quad(1,1,1,1)
\end{gathered}
$$
