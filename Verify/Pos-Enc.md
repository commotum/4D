This project is a polished research library, built on nanoGPT, for studying transformers as **next-state predictors**, not just next-token predictors. The core problem is that positional encoding in transformers has fractured into domain-specific solutions: standard RoPE works naturally for 1D autoregression, but once we move to spatiotemporal data there is no widely accepted universal positional scheme that cleanly spans multiple modalities. To study that gap in a controlled way, the library generates tiny procedural worlds—simple recurrences and cellular automata across **0D+t, 1D+t, 2D+t, and 3D+t**—so that the cleanest possible experiments still force a model to infer a latent rule and predict its evolution.

The goal is to compare positional encodings fairly, under a shared backbone and matched budgets, and ask which one gives transformers the best geometric prior both within a single domain and in a mixed-domain setting. We ablate standard RoPE, axial RoPE at different dimensionalities, and **MonSTER**, a 4D spacetime generalization of RoPE that uses Lorentz-compatible block transforms over **(t, x, y, z)** to preserve RoPE’s absolute-to-relative structure while providing one shared geometric language across modalities. By keeping vocabularies tiny, tasks synthetic, and outputs framed as next-state prediction, the project isolates spatiotemporal structure instead of burying it under language, search, or task-specific engineering—and tests whether a genuinely domain-agnostic positional encoder for a domain-agnostic transformer is possible.
