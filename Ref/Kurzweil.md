Signals go up and down the conceptual hierarchy. A signal going up means, "I've detected a pattern." A signal going down means, "I'm expecting your pattern to occur," and is essentially a prediction. Both upward and downward signals can be either excitatory, or inhibitory. Each pattern is itself in a particular order, and is not readily reversed. Even if a pattern appears to have multidimensional aspects, it is represented by a one dimensional sequence of lower level patterns. A pattern is an ordered sequence of other patterns, so each recognizer is inherently recursive. There can be many levels of hierarchy.
